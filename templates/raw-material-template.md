## How to use this document

This is not a polished document. It's a working file. Dump things in here as they come up during sessions, dictation, or offline thinking. Organize loosely by section. Don't over-structure it. The goal is to never lose a good idea or detail because it didn't have a place to go yet.

**When material gets used in a chapter:** Move it to the Spent Material Log at the bottom with a note about which chapter used it. This prevents accidentally re-using material and keeps a record of what went where.

**When to add to this file:** During planning sessions when you surface material that belongs in a later chapter. During dictation sessions when you go on a tangent that's good but not for the current chapter. Whenever you think of something and don't want to forget it.

## Real Example: Raw Material from *Too Many Goats on the Mountain*

The TMG raw material file went through 9 versions and contained everything from one-liner character details to full epilogue structural plans. Here are examples of what each section looked like in practice.

### Character Details (as written)

> **Bernie (last name TBD)**
> First real VP of Sales. Absolute character. Ex-Oracle, sales guy through and through. His best line was always: "I started in engineering, but had to move to sales because my hair was just too good." Truth was... he was right.
>
> *Status: Not yet in manuscript. Available for hyper-growth chapters or epilogue.*

### Story Fragment (as written)

> **Marc Baumgartner Arc**
> The throughline: Marc threads from the first company through the fourth. Introduced early as one of the first sales hires. Needs at least one more mention during the growth period so his reappearance later doesn't come out of nowhere. He comes back in two important ways, and his story is also a "small valley" callback.
>
> *Potential placement: Hiring chapter (back-channel reference), Europe expansion chapter (business partnership), end of scouting trip (personal grace note, best day on a mountain).*

### Epilogue Material (as written)

> **The Three Principles**
> 1. The CEO IS the company.
> 2. The valley is small. The connections run deep.
> 3. There are many shades of gray.
>
> Framing: the style guide says no preachy, no how-to. But the epilogue is the one place where stepping outside that frame might work, because the whole book earned it by NOT doing it.

### Spent Material Log (as written)

> - **Vegas Camera Story** → Used in Chapter 6. Locked.
> - **Steve's "Take the Beach" Speech** → Used in Chapter 10. Locked.

### What makes this example work

The status notes on every entry prevent confusion about what's been used and what's still available. The story fragment includes potential placement, not just the raw material. The epilogue section captures the structural approach (the framing idea) alongside the content itself. And the spent material log creates a clear boundary between live material and material that's already in the book.
