## How to use this template

1. **Use this when you have three or more chapters that form a single narrative arc.** If the chapters are relatively standalone, individual DraftGuides are sufficient.

2. **Write the arc spine before any individual DraftGuides.** The spine establishes the through-line. Individual DraftGuides fill in the per-chapter detail within that structure.

3. **The arc spine is the source of truth.** If a DraftGuide conflicts with the arc spine, the spine wins. If something in the arc spine needs to change, update the spine first, then cascade the change to the DraftGuides.

4. **Thread tracking is the most important section.** The whole point of an arc spine is making sure threads planted in chapter one actually land in chapter three. Without the tracking table, threads get dropped.

## Real Example: Seqera Arc from *Too Many Goats on the Mountain*

This is a condensed version of the actual arc spine used to plan five chapters covering the author's time at a European biotech software startup. You can give this entire template, including this example, to Claude and say: "Here's what another project did. Help me build one for my book."

### Theme

**The author's operational pattern-matching worked. His strategic pattern-matching was the blind spot. The company around what he built couldn't turn it into enough.**

Pattern recognition as superpower *and* blind spot. Both at once, at different altitudes. The skill was real across five companies. It built a functioning customer operation from zero. What it couldn't do was rescue a company whose internal theories of itself were structurally incompatible.

The reader should walk away feeling: *He was great at what he did. The company he did it inside couldn't become what it needed to become. That's a different kind of ending than any of the prior four.*

### Structural Forces

1. **European vs. US** — Time zones, work culture, vacation law, customer concentration. The European startup that couldn't serve US enterprise the way US enterprise expects.
2. **Open source vs. enterprise** — The founders' commitment to a free-first flywheel colliding with the commercial team's push. The company had incompatible theories of its own business, all held in good faith.

Both forces appear in every chapter, escalating from Ch30 (perks and opportunities) to Ch34 (existential pressures).

### Chapter Summaries

| Ch | Title | What it's about | Words |
|---|---|---|---|
| 30 | Déjà Vu | The yes. Fifth time saying yes to a startup, with one blind spot he doesn't know he has. | ~2,400 |
| 31 | Bienvenido a Seqera | First real look at the company. Spain itself. The offsite as a window into people, culture, and tensions. | ~2,500 |
| 32 | Wow, It's Early | Reality hitting. Applying the playbook. The function getting built. | ~4,000 |
| 33 | Free Is a Great Business Model Until It Isn't | The collision. Both structural forces become the text. Three functions, three angles, same problem. | ~3,150 |
| 34 | Biotech Winter | Everything converging. External forces, internal contradictions, personal. Pattern recognition works at exactly the moment it can't save anything. | ~3,500 |

### Escalation Table

| Chapter | European/US shows up as | Open Source vs. Enterprise shows up as |
|---|---|---|
| 30 | A perk (Barcelona) | An opportunity (reason #1 for saying yes) |
| 31 | Dress code, time zones, dinner culture | Roadmap tension, product philosophy |
| 32 | 5:30 AM Slack, daily reality | Support tickets tell the story, no implementations |
| 33 | Vacation law, August shutdown, work-hours gap | Becomes the text (three functions, three angles) |
| 34 | Jurisdictional unwind | Customers go back to free |

### Hard Outs

- **Named lost customer** — OUT. Bundled into broader market framing.
- **Succession tension between two leaders** — OUT. Land as "succession worked, both promoted."
- **Specific financial numbers** — OUT. No ARR, board targets, or runway figures.

### What makes this example work

The theme sentence does real work. It's not "the Seqera chapters." It's "pattern recognition as superpower and blind spot." Every chapter in the arc serves that theme. The structural forces table shows how the same two tensions escalate across five chapters without repeating. The hard outs prevent scope creep and protect real people. The thread tracking (omitted here for length) ensured that things planted in Ch30 actually landed in Ch34.
